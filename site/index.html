💡 The Problem

80% of Vietnamese students are afraid to speak English. They get only 5 minutes of speaking practice per week in class. Native tutors cost 500K-1M VND/hour — unaffordable for most families.

🎯 Our Solution

An AI conversation partner that students can talk to 24/7, get real-time grammar + pronunciation feedback, and improve at 1/10th the cost of a human tutor.

Vietnamese Accent Handling

Common ASR challenges for Vietnamese speakers:

  • /θ/ → /t/: "think" transcribed as "tink"
  • /ð/ → /d/: "the" transcribed as "de"
  • /v/ → /b/: "very" transcribed as "bery"
  • /r/ ↔ /l/: "right" transcribed as "light"
  • Final consonants dropped: "good" transcribed as "goo"

Grammar Correction Engine

Multi-layer grammar checking pipeline:

1
Rule-Based Patterns (10ms) — Catches common Vietnamese learner errors: "I very like" → "I like ... very much"
2
Language Tool API (200ms) — Comprehensive grammar, spelling, punctuation checking
3
LLM Grammar Check (1s) — Context-aware, nuanced corrections for complex sentences

Common Error Categories for Vietnamese Learners

CategoryExampleCorrectionExplanation (Vietnamese)
Tense "I go yesterday" "I went yesterday" Dùng thì Quá khứ đơn (went) vì có "yesterday"
Article "I want apple" "I want an apple" Cần mạo từ "an" trước danh từ đếm được số ít
Word Order "I very like English" "I like English very much" "Very" không đứng trước động từ
Subject-Verb "He don't like it" "He doesn't like it" He/She/It đi với "doesn't"
Vocabulary "I want to eat pho please" "I'd like to have pho, please" Dùng "I'd like" cho lịch sự hơn
